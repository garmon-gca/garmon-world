# Contact

Garmon is currently in an early public research stage.

The best contact style is calm technical feedback:

- specific;
- low-pressure;
- public-safe;
- focused on the review surface rather than private internals.

The goal of contact is not broad validation.

The goal is clearer review of the current public-safe achievement and the boundaries around it.

## Current Public Focus

The clearest current public-safe achievement is:

> Body Shadow and body-contour distinction before speech.

The public materials also describe:

- the static choice boundary chain;
- the synthetic pre-selected result boundary candidate;
- memory as trace rather than hidden motor;
- speech as expression layer rather than source of choice;
- answer origin before speech as the central research direction.

Useful contact helps make these ideas easier to review without exposing the protected mechanism.

## Good Reasons To Reach Out

You can reach out if you want to discuss:

- whether the Body Shadow / body-contour distinction framing is understandable;
- what a small body-contour artifact should show;
- whether the static choice boundary chain is understandable as bounded public-safe evidence;
- whether the pre-selected result boundary candidate is clearly synthetic and closed;
- how to make a small artifact or demo more reviewable;
- how to evaluate the event-reading step before LLM speech;
- how to keep public claims clear without hiding the achievement;
- how to explain the boundary between state, meaning, memory, choice, behavior, truth, evidence, and speech;
- how to keep memory/trace language from sounding like active authority;
- how to keep the LLM clearly framed as the speech layer rather than the source of choice;
- how to improve public-safe technical explanations;
- relevant papers, projects, benchmarks, or review methods;
- how to make the public surface clearer without making the claims stronger.

The most useful feedback is usually narrow and concrete.

## Preferred First Message

A useful first message can be simple:

> I read the Garmon public notes. I am interested in the Body Shadow / body-contour distinction question. Here is one suggestion or question.

Another useful form:

> The public framing was clear to me up to this point: [...]. The part I would simplify is [...].

A useful static-chain-focused message can be:

> I understood the static choice boundary chain as a bounded review surface, not as a claim of live choice. The boundary I would clarify is [...].

A useful memory-focused message can be:

> I understood that memory/trace is not being claimed as active authority. The wording I would make clearer is [...].

A useful artifact-focused message can be:

> I think the first public artifact should compare the same event under several body contours before final speech.

## What To Avoid

Please avoid:

- asking for private project material;
- asking for raw internal logs;
- asking for unpublished local files;
- asking for exact private implementation details;
- asking for protected internal identifiers;
- asking for private runtime material;
- asking for private diagnostic material;
- asking for private configuration material;
- treating static/shadow evidence as live behavior;
- treating the static choice boundary chain as live choice;
- treating the pre-selected result boundary candidate as an actual selected result;
- treating memory or residue as active authority;
- treating speech as the source of choice;
- framing the project as a finished product;
- framing Garmon as conscious, alive, or maturely autonomous;
- pushing for strong claims before a public artifact exists;
- asking for disclosure of the protected mechanism.

The public surface is intended for review.

It is not intended as full disclosure of the private contour.

## Public Boundary Reminder

The central public law is:

```text

```

- body contour may participate without becoming command;
- meaning may participate without becoming selected result;
- memory/trace may participate without becoming hidden motor;
- speech may participate without becoming the source of choice;
- evidence may participate without becoming permission or truth.

Good contact helps preserve this boundary.
