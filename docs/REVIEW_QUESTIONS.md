# Review Questions

These questions are for technical readers, reviewers, and careful outsiders.

The goal is not to push Garmon into stronger claims.

The goal is to make the public review surface clearer, more useful, and more honest.

Garmon studies the order in which an artificial answer may receive an origin before speech. A good review should help clarify that idea without exposing private implementation details and without giving any layer more authority than it has.

## Main Framing

- Is Garmon understandable as a public research preview rather than a finished product?
- Is the central idea clear: an answer may have an origin before speech?
- Is it clear that Garmon studies a pre-speech path, not only final text generation?
- Is it clear that the LLM is treated as a speech layer, not as the whole system?
- Is the phrase “behavior before speech” understandable without sounding mystical?
- Does the public framing show a real idea without turning it into hype?
- Does the public framing avoid sounding defensive or apologetic?
- Does the text make Garmon look like a normal chatbot, agent wrapper, or prompt system when it is not?
- Does the text make clear that speech expresses a path rather than creating the path after the fact?

## Answer Origin

- Is the question “where did the answer come from?” clear to an outside reader?
- Does the material explain why a useful final answer is not enough to understand answer origin?
- Is the difference clear between a text task and an event that meets an internal state?
- Does the public text show why final wording should not be treated as the whole behavior?
- Is the chain from state to speech understandable?
- Which part of the chain needs the clearest public explanation?
- Does the public material show why answer origin matters for review, safety, continuity, and future artifacts?

## Layer Boundaries

Garmon depends on layer separation.

A useful review should check whether each layer keeps its role.

- Is it clear that internal state is not command authority?
- Is it clear that body contour is not behavior?
- Is it clear that meaning candidate is not selected result?
- Is it clear that memory or residue is not a hidden motor?
- Is it clear that observation is not the owner of truth?
- Is it clear that evidence is not permission?
- Is it clear that speech is not the source of choice?
- Is it clear that visibility is not authority?
- Is it clear that a report is not decision truth?
- Is the law “participation is not power” understandable and useful?
- Where does the wording accidentally give a layer more authority than it should have?

- Which body-contour candidates are easiest to understand publicly: quiet baseline, fatigue, recovery, stress, mixed stress/recovery, or day/night rhythm?
- What comparison would best show that different contours do not collapse into one generic label?

- Does the material explain why body contour matters before speech?

- What should a minimal body-contour artifact show first?

## Final Review Question

The most important review question is:

> Does the public surface make it possible to understand Garmon’s central idea without mistaking the current evidence for a finished live system?

A good public page should hold both sides:

- Garmon is not empty.
- Garmon is not overclaimed.

The work is strongest when it says exactly what it can hold.
